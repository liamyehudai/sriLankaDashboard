March 2026 Crisis: The Strait of Hormuz

ACTIVE ALERT

The Global Chokepoint: Recent regional conflict has led to the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz. This narrow 33km waterway normally handles ~20% of the global oil supply, and crucially, over 80% of Asia's crude oil imports pass through it.

The Threat to Sri Lanka: As a nation that imports nearly 100% of its fuel, the impact is devastating. The state-run Sapugaskanda refinery relies entirely on Middle Eastern crude. The closure has severed supply chains and triggered a massive surge in global spot market prices.

Supply Dependency

Sri Lanka's foreign reserves sit at roughly $6.8B—only enough to fund ~3 months of critical fuel, food, and medicine imports at surging prices.

Emergency Rationing

To conserve dwindling supplies, the government has been forced to mandate a nationwide 4-day workweek and strictly limit nighttime EV charging.

The Solarpunk Imperative: This severe external shock brutally highlights the dangers of the bottom 15% "Oil" layer in the generation mix. Accelerating the transition to localized Solar and Wind is no longer just an environmental goal; it is a matter of absolute national survival and economic sovereignty.
